digital certificate

a digital certificate is simply a digital signature binding an entity to some public-key. to be concrete, say a party Charlie has generated a key-pair for a secure digital signature scheme (we are only concerned with signature schemes satisfying def-sig-existentially-unforgeable). assume further that another party Bob has also generated a key-pair (in the present discussion, these may be keys for either a signature scheme or a public-key encryption scheme), and that Charlie knows that is Bob's public key. then Charlie can compute the signature

and give this signature to Bob. we call a certificate for Bob's key issued by Charlie. in practice a certificate should unambiguously identify the party holding a particular public key and so a more uniquely descriptive term than "Bob" would be used, for example, Bob's full name and email address, or the URL of Bob's website.

Bob can send to Alice, who can then verify that is indeed a valid signature on the message 'Bob's key is ' with respect to . assuming verification succeeds, Alice now knows that Charlie has signed the indicated message. if Alice trusts Charlie, she can accept as Bob's legitimate public key.
all communication between Bob and Alice can occur over an insecure and unauthenticated channel. if an active adversary interferes with the transmission of from Bob to Alice, that adversary will be unable to generate a valid certificate linking Bob to any other public key unless Charlie had previously signed some other certificate linking Bob with (in which case this is anyway not much of an attack). this all assumes that Charlie is not dishonest and that his private key has not been compromised.
we have omitted many details in the above description. most prominently, we have not discussed how Alice learns in the first place; how Charlie can be sure that is Bob's public key; and how Alice decides whether to trust Charlie. fully specifying such details (and others) defines a public-key infrastructure that enables the widespread distribution of public keys.
