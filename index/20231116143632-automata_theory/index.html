automata theory

automata theory deals with the definitions and properties of mathematical models of computation. these models play a role in several applied areas of computer science. one model, called the finite automaton, is used in text processing, compilers, and hardware design. another model, called the context-free grammar, is used in programming languages and artificial intelligence. automata theory is an excellent place to begin the study of the theory of computation. the theories of computability and complexity require a precise definition of a computer. automata theory allows practice with formal definitions of computation as it introduces concepts relevant to other nontheoretical areas of computer science. (Sipser 2012)
