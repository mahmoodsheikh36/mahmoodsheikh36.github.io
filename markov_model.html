markov model

/markov model/s are the class of probabilistic models that make the markov assumption, that is, they assume the markov property, that, they assume we can predict the probability of some future unit without looking too far in the past.

a Markov model is a stochastic model used to model pseudo-randomly changing systems. it is assumed that future states depend only on the current state, not on the events that occurred before it (that is, it assumes the Markov property). generally, this assumption enables reasoning and computation with the model that would otherwise be intractable. for this reason, in the fields of predictive modelling and probabilistic forecasting, it is desirable for a given model to exhibit the Markov property.
