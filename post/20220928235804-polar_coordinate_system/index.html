a 2d coordinate system
points in the \(x,y\) plane are described not by \((x,y)\) coordinates but by \((r,\theta)\) coordinates, where \(r\) is the magnitude and \(\theta\) is the angle between the vector and the \(x\) axis

\begin{align*} r &= \sqrt{x^2+y^2}\\ \theta &= \arctan\left(\frac{y}{x}\right) \end{align*}

the base vectors of this coordinate system are usually denoted by \(\rhat\) and \(\thetahat\)

\begin{align*} \rhat(\theta) &= \cos\theta\ihat + \sin\theta\jhat\\ \thetahat(\theta) &= -\sin\theta\ihat + \cos\theta\jhat \end{align*}



1 from polar to cartesian coordinates

\begin{align*} x &= r\cos(\theta)\\ y &= r\sin(\theta) \end{align*}
