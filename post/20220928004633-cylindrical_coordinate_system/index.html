Cylindrical coordinate system

September 28, 2022

the \(z\) axis of the cylindrical system is identical to that of the cartesian coordinate system, however points in the \(x-y\) plane are described not by \(x-y\) coordinates but by \(r-\theta\) coordinates where \(r\) is the distance from the origin and \(\theta\) is the angle between \(r\) and the \(x\) axis
we see that

\begin{align*} r &= \sqrt{x^2+y^2}\\ \theta &= \arctan\left(\frac{y}{x}\right) \end{align*}
