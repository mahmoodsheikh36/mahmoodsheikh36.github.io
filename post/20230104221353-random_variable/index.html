a random variable X is a function \(X(\Omega)\) from the sample space to the real line (\(\mathbb{R}\)), such that every event in the sample space corresponds to a real number. \(P_i\) is the probability of receiving the value \(X_i\) and \(P(X)\) is the probability function

we roll a symmetric coin 3 times
\[ \Omega = \{(h,h,h),(h,h,t),(h,t,h),(t,h,h),(t,h,t),(h,t,t),(t,t,h),(t,t,t)\} \]
we define \(X\) as the number of times that “heads” was received
\(X\) is a random variable with 4 possible values, \(X(\Omega)=0,1,2,3\), \(P(X=0)=\frac{1}{8},P(X=1)=\frac{3}{8},P(X=2)=\frac{3}{8},P(X=3)=\frac{1}{8}\)

let \(f_i\) be the frequency of the value \(x_i\), we get \(\sum_{i}^{k} f_i = n\), so \(\overline{x} = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{k} x_if_i}{n}\)
let \(p_i=\frac{f_i}{n}\) be the probability to get the value \(x_i\), so \(\sum_{i}^{k} p_i=1\), we get \(\overline{x}=\frac{\sum_{1}^{k} x_if_i}{n}=\frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{k} x_if_i=\sum_{i=1}^{k} x_i \cdot \frac{f_i}{n}=\sum_{i=1}^{k} x_ip_i\)
\[ \overline{x} = \sum_{i=1}^{k} x_ip_i \]

let \(x_1,x_2,\dots,x_k\) be arbitrary values and \(f_1,f_2,\dots,f_k\) their frequencies
the variances of \(x_1,x_2,\dots,x_k\) is the measure of their scattering around the average
\[ S^2 = \frac{1}{n} \sum_{i=1}^{k} (x_i-\overline{x})^2 f_i \]

we build off from the previous lemmas:

\begin{align*} S^2 &= \frac{1}{n} \sum_{i=1}^{k} (x_i-\overline{x})^2 f_i\\ &= \sum_{i=1}^{k} (x_i^2-2x_i\overline{x}+\overline{x}^2) \cdot \frac{f_i}{n}\\ &= \sum_{i=1}^{k} (x_i^2-2x_i\overline{x}+\overline{x}^2) \cdot p_i\\ &= \sum_{i=1}^{k} x_i^2p_i - 2\sum_{i=1}^{k} x_ip_i\overline{x} + \sum_{i=1}^{k} \overline{x}^2p_i\\ &= \sum_{i=1}^{k} x_i^2p_i - 2\overline{x}\sum_{i=1}^{k} x_ip_i + \overline{x}^2\sum_{i=1}^{k} p_i\\ &= \sum_{i=1}^{k} x_i^2p_i - 2\overline{x}^2 + \overline{x}^2\\ &= \sum_{i=1}^{k} x_i^2p_i - \overline{x}^2 \end{align*}

so
\[ S^2 = \sum_{i=1}^{k} x_i^2p_i - \overline{x}^2 \]

\(E(x)\) is the expected value of a random variable \(X\), which is the average of it, so we get \(\overline{x} = \sum_{i=1}^{k} x_ip_i\) so \(E(x) = \sum_{i=1}^{k} x_ip_i\)

on his way to work a person goes through 3 traffic lights, the probability of the first being green is 0.6, the second 0.5, the third 0.4
let \(x\) be the number of traffic lights they pass without waiting (green)

find the probability function of the random variable \(x\)

\(x\) can equal one of \(0,1,2,3\)
\(p(x=0)=0.4 \cdot 0.5 \cdot 0.6 = 0.12\) (no green)
\(p(x=1)=0.6 \cdot 0.6 \cdot 0.5 \cdot 0.6 + 0.4 \cdot 0.5 \cdot 0.6 + 0.4 \cdot 0.5 \cdot 0.4 = 0.38\) (1 green)
\(p(x=2)=0.6 \cdot 0.5 \cdot 0.6 + 0.4 \cdot 0.5 \cdot 0.4 + 0.6 \cdot 0.5 \cdot 0.4 = 0.38\) (2 green)
\(p(x=3)=0.6 \cdot 0.5 \cdot 0.4 = 0.12\) (3 green)
so \(p(x) = \begin{cases} 0.12 \quad x=0\\ 0.38 \quad x=1\\ 0.38 \quad x=2\\ 0.12 \quad x=3 \end{cases}\)

find the expected value and the variance of \(x\)

\(\mathrm{E}(x)=0.12 \cdot 0 + 0.38 \cdot 1 + 0.38 \cdot 2 + 0.12 \cdot 3 = 1.5\)
\(\mathrm{E}(x)=0.12 \cdot 0^2 + 0.38 \cdot 1^2 + 0.38 \cdot 2^2 + 0.12 \cdot 3^2 = 2.98\)
\(\mathrm{Var}(x)=\mathrm{E}(x^2)-\mathrm{E}(x)^2=2.98-(1.5)^2=2.98-2.25=0.73\)
