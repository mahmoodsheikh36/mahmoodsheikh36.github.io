1 exercises on permutations

in how many ways can we seat 5 soldiers in a row?

5! = 120

in how many ways can we seat those 5 soldiers in a row such that their commander is always on the left?

4! = 24

given the letters a,b,c,d,e,f, how many words can we build from these letters

if a,b,e cant be the last letter in the word

\(6! - 5! - 5! - 5!\)

if a has to be the first letter and b,e cant be the last letter in the word

5! - 4! - 4!

how many numbers of 5 digits can we make from the digits of 41589 than are bigger than 41589?

the total permutations is \(5!=120\)
for the number to be smaller we only have to stick the digit 1 to the left, any other operation will make the number bigger
sticking 1 to the left means we dont care about it, the permutations we get is 4!
so the anser is \(5! - 4! - 1 = 95\), note that we decrement by 1 because we want to exclude the number itself too

2 general problems (mixed)

given 8 similar white balls and 7 different colorful balls and 20 cells, how many options do we have of inserting the balls with the condition:

every cell gets at most a single ball

every cell gets at most a single ball \(\implies\) no repetition
8 white balls \(\implies\) order doesnt matter \(\implies\) combination \(\frac{20!}{(20-8)!8!}\)
for colored balls 12 cells are left, order does matter so its a variation \(\implies\) \(\frac{12!}{(12-7)!}\)
so the answer is \(\frac{20!}{(20-8)!8!} \cdot \frac{12!}{(12-7)!}\)

every cell gets at most 1 white ball without limit on colored balls as long as a white ball and a colored ball cant be in the same cell

every cell gets at most a single white ball \(\implies\) combination \(\implies\) \(\frac{20!}{(20-8)!8!}\)
no limit on colored balls \(\implies\) variation with repetition \(\implies\) \(12^7\)
so the answer is \(\frac{20!}{(20-8)!8!} \cdot 12^7\)

every cell gets at most 1 white ball without limit on colored balls (a white ball and a colored ball can be in the same cell)

every cell gets at most a single white ball \(\implies\) combination \(\implies\) \(\frac{20!}{(20-8)!8!}\)
no limit on colored balls \(\implies\) variation with repetition \(\implies 20^7\)
so the answer is \(\frac{20!}{12!8!} \cdot 20^7\)

3 exercises on circular permutations

how many options do we have of seating 8 people on 8 similar seats around a circular table with the condition:

no limits

8 different people \(\implies\) order matters, repetition isnt allowed \(\implies\) combination \(\implies 8!/8 = 7\)

such that a single person reserves a chair for themselves

\(7!\)

such that a single chair is colored red

\(8!\)

chairs are all colored differently

\(8!\)

8 people sitting around a table, person1 and person2 have to be seated next to each other, how many is there, with the condition:

person3 and person4 cant sit next to each other

\(6! \cdot 2 - 5! \cdot 2 \cdot 2 = 960\)

person 3 and person 2 cant sit next to each other

\(6! \cdot 2 - 5! \cdot 2 = 1200\)

4 exercises on permutation with repetition

how many words of 7 letters can we generate from the letters aaabbgd

\(\frac{7!}{3! \cdot 2! \cdot 1 \cdot 1} = 420\)

if the word starts with the letter b

\(\frac{6!}{3!} = 120\)

if the word starts with the letter b and ends with a

\(\frac{5!}{2} = 60\)

how many options do we have of placing in a circle 13 similar balls 6 of which are red, 4 yellow, 2 blue, 1 green

\(\frac{13!}{6! \cdot 4! \cdot 2 \cdot 1} / 13 = 13860\)

such that the blue balls are next to each other

\(\frac{12!}{6! \cdot 4! \cdot 1} / 12 = 2310\)

how many ways can we divide 9 dancers into 3 groups of 3?

every group is \(3!\), the groups themselves thats another \(3!\), so the answer is \(9! / 3! / 3! / 3! / 3!\)
