rational root theorem

\(\DeclareMathOperator{\spn}{span}\) \(\DeclareMathOperator{\dom}{domain}\) \(\DeclareMathOperator{\ran}{range}\) \(\DeclareMathOperator{\rng}{range}\) \(\DeclareMathOperator{\img}{Im}\) \(\DeclareMathOperator{\adj}{adj}\) \(\newcommand\dif[1]{\:\textrm{d}#1}\) \(\DeclarePairedDelimiter\ceil{\lceil}{\rceil}\) \(\DeclarePairedDelimiter\floor{\lfloor}{\rfloor}\)

the rational root theorem states a constraint on rational solutions of a polynomial \(a_{n}x^{n}+a_{n-1}x^{n-1}+\cdots+a_{0} = 0\) with integer coefficients \(a_{i}\in \mathbb {Z}\) and \(a_{0}, a_{n}\neq 0\)
the theorem states that each rational solution \(x = p⁄q\), written in lowest terms so that \(p\) and \(q\) are relatively prime, satisfies:

  • \(p\) is an integer factor of the constant term \(a_0\), and
  • \(q\) is an integer factor of the leading coefficient \(a_n\).
