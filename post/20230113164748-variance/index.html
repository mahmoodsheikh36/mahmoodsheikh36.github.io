let \(X\) be a random variable whose expected value is finite \(\mu=\mathrm{E}(X)\), the variance of \(X\), denoted by \(\mathrm{Var}(x)\), is defined as
\[ \mathrm{Var}(x) = \mathrm{E}\left(\left(X-\mu\right)^2\right) \]

let \(X\) be a random variable whose distribution law is
\[ x = \begin{cases} -1 & 1/4\\ 0 & 1/4\\ 1 & 1/2 \end{cases} \]
we find the variance:

\begin{gather*} \mu = \mathrm{E}(X) = -1 \cdot \frac{1}{4} + 0 \cdot \frac{1}{4} + 1 \cdot \frac{1}{2} = \frac{1}{4}\\ \mathrm{Var}(X) = \mathrm{E}\left(\left(X-\frac{1}{4}\right)^2\right) = \left(-1-\frac{1}{4}\right)^2 \cdot \frac{1}{4} + \left(0-\frac{1}{4}\right)^2 \cdot \frac{1}{4} + \left(1-\frac{1}{4}\right) \cdot \frac{1}{2} = \frac{11}{16} \end{gather*}

\(\mathrm{Var}(X) \geq 0\)

\(\mathrm{Var}(x)\) iff \(X\) is constant

\(\mathrm{Var}(aX+b)=a^2 \cdot \mathrm{Var}(X)\)
