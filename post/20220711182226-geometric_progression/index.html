geometric progression

\(\DeclareMathOperator{\spn}{span}\) \(\DeclareMathOperator{\dom}{domain}\) \(\DeclareMathOperator{\ran}{range}\) \(\DeclareMathOperator{\rng}{range}\) \(\DeclareMathOperator{\img}{Im}\) \(\DeclareMathOperator{\adj}{adj}\) \(\newcommand\dif[1]{\:\textrm{d}#1}\) \(\DeclarePairedDelimiter\ceil{\lceil}{\rceil}\) \(\DeclarePairedDelimiter\floor{\lfloor}{\rfloor}\)

a geometric progression is a sequence of non-zero numbers where each term after the first is found by multiplying the previous one by a fixed, non-zero number called the common ratio
the formula for the $n$th number in a geometric sequence with initial value \(a\) and common ratio \(r\) is:
\[ a_{n}=ar^{n-1} \]
