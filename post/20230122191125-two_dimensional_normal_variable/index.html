two dimensional random variable

math

the pair \((X,Y)\) is a random variable if \(X\) and \(Y\) are defined over the same probability space
refer to joint probability distribution

let \((X,Y)\) be a two dimensional random variable, the probability function (called marginal distribution) of \(X\) is defined as \[ P(X=x)=\sum_{y} P(X=x,Y=y) \], and of \(Y\) is defined as \(P(Y=y)=\sum_{x} P(X=x,Y=y)\)

let \((X,Y)\) be a two dimensional random variable, the conditional probability function of \(X\) for \(Y=y\) is \[ P(X=x,Y=y)=\frac{P(X=x,Y=y)}{P(Y=y)} \] and similarly, \[ P(Y=y|X=x)=\frac{P(X=x,Y=y)}{P(X=x)} \]

we throw a symmetric coin with the values 0 and 1, if we get 0 we throw a die, if we get 1 we throw the same coin again.
we define \(X\) as the result of the first throw and \(Y\) as the result of the second
we build a joint distribution table

what is the probability that in the first throw we got 0 if we know in the second throw we got a number smaller than 3?

\begin{gather*} P(X=0|Y<3)=\frac{0+\frac{1}{12}+\frac{1}{12}}{\frac{1}{4}+\frac{4}{12}+\frac{1}{12}}\\ E(X)=0 \cdot \frac{1}{2} + 1 \cdot \frac{1}{2} = \frac{1}{2}\\ E(Y)=0 \cdot \frac{1}{4} + 1 \cdot \frac{4}{12} + 2 \cdot \frac{1}{12} + 3 \cdot \frac{1}{12} + 4 \cdot \frac{1}{12} + 5 \cdot \frac{1}{12} + 6 \cdot \frac{1}{12} = 2\\ \mathrm{Var}(X)=E(X^2)-(E(X))^2=0^2 \cdot \frac{1}{2} + 1^2 \cdot \frac{1}{2} - \left(\frac{1}{2}\right) = \frac{1}{4}\\ \vdots \end{gather*}

given \((X,Y)\) is a two dimensional random variable, \(X\) and \(Y\) are independent if
\[ P(X=x,Y=y)=P(X=x) \cdot P(Y=y) \]
for all values of \(X\) and \(Y\)

let \((X,Y)\) be a two dimensional random variable, let \(f(X,Y)\) be a function of \(X\) and \(Y\), then
\[ E(f(X,Y)) = \sum_{x} \sum_{y} f(x,y) \cdot P(X=x,Y=y) \]

let \((X,Y)\) be a two dimensional random variable
we know \(E(X+Y)=E(X)+E(Y)\), we denote \(E(Y)=\mu_2,E(X)=\mu_1\)

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Var}(X+Y) &= E\left(\left(X+Y-\mu_1-\mu_2\right)^2\right)\\ &= E\left((X-\mu_1)^2+(Y-\mu_2^2)+2(X-\mu_1)(Y-\mu_2)\right)\\ &= E\left((X-\mu_1)^2\right)+E\left(\left(Y-\mu_2\right)^2\right)+2E((X-\mu_1)(Y-\mu_2))\\ &= \mathrm{Var}(X)+\mathrm{Var}(Y)+2E((X-E(X))(Y-E(Y))) \end{align*}
