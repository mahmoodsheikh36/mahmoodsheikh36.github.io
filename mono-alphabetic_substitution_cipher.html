mono-alphabetic substitution cipher

a cipher in which an alphabet is mapped onto another, the key space is any bijection.

assume English-language text is being encrypted (i.e., the text is grammatically correct English writing, not just text written using characters of the English alphabet). the mono-alphabetic substitution cipher can then be attacked by utilizing statistical patterns of the English language. (of course, the same attack works for any language.) the attack relies on the facts that:

  1. for any key, the mapping of each letter is fixed, and so if is mapped to , then every appearance of in the plaintext will result in the appearance of in the ciphertext.
  2. the frequency distribution of individual letters in the English language is known (english letter frequency distribution). of course, very short texts may deviate from this distribution, but even texts consisting of only a few sentences tend to have distributions that are very close to the average.
