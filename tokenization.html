tokenization

tokens are the atomic (indivisible) units of text. each time step corresponds to 1 token, but what precisely constitutes a token is a design choice. For example, we could represent the sentence "baby needs a new pair of shoes" as a sequence of 7 words, where the set of all words comprise a large vocabulary (typically tens or hundreds of thousands of words). or we would represent the same sentence as a much longer sequence of 30 characters, using a much smaller vocabulary (there are only 256 distinct ASCII characters).
