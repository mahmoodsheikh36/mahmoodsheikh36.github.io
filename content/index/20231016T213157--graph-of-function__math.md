it is convenient to denote a point in the graph of a function <img src="/ltximg/98d541edf19.svg" alt="\(\brm{f}: \mathbb{R}^k \to \mathbb{R}^{n-k}\)" style="height: 1.0467em; vertical-align: -0.0619em; display: inline-block" class="org-latex org-latex-inline" /> as <img src="/ltximg/3d62a00b480.svg" alt="\(\pmqty{\brm{x}\\ \brm{f}(\brm{x})}\)" style="height: 2.6268em; vertical-align: -0.5392em; display: inline-block" class="org-latex org-latex-inline" />. with <img src="/ltximg/cb4bbed82df.svg" alt="\(\brm{x} \in \mathbb{R}^k\)" style="height: 1.0467em; vertical-align: -0.0619em; display: inline-block" class="org-latex org-latex-inline" /> and <img src="/ltximg/097e8239126.svg" alt="\(\brm{f}(\brm{x}) \in \mathbb{R}^{n-k}\)" style="height: 1.2741em; vertical-align: -0.2893em; display: inline-block" class="org-latex org-latex-inline" />. but this presupposes that the "active" variables are the first variables, which is a problem, since we usually cannot use the same active variables at all points of the mainfold. <br/>
