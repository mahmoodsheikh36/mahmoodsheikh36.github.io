15 Digital abstraction
how do we represent the values 0 and 1 using a digital logic circuits?
on one side the logic circuits have only 2 variables, voltage and time and they can have any value in a given
range
and on the other side, boolean algebra has only 2 values which are the constants 0 and 1
the solution:
• voltage represents logical value, for example, a high voltage represents 1 and a low voltage represents 0,
other voltages, for example a voltage between “high” and “low” are deﬁned as not represnting of logical
values
• sometimes, it is agreed upon that the output of a circuit represents the value of the switching function,
other times its agreed upon that it doesnt
15.1 Logical levels
the two logical values 0 and 1 are represented in Electronic circuits using logical levels.
an electronic circuit receives electrical energy from a power supply like a battery, the power supply is the
high voltage Vplus and the low voltage is called Vminus
the levels of voltages denoted v in a circuit is limited in the range V minus ≤ v ≤ V plus, the amplitude of
voltages is deﬁned as ∆v = V plus − V minus
as an example:
V plus − 0.2∆v = 4volt ≤ v ≤ 5volt = V plus represents logical 1
V minus = 0volt ≤ v ≤ 1volt = V minus + 0.2∆v represents logical 0
