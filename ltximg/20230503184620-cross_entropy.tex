\textbf{cross-entropy}, written as \(H(P,Q)\), is a kind of measure of dissimilarity between two distributions \(P\) and \(Q\). the general definition is\\
\[
H(P,Q) = \brm{E}_{\brm{z} \sim P(\brm{Z})}j[\log Q(\brm{z})] = \int P(\brm{z})\log Q(\brm{z}) \dif{\brm{z}}
\]
